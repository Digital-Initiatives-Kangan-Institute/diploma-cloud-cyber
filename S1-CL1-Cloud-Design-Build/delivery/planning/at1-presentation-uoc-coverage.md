# AT1 — UoC coverage, and the presentation (Part B) subset

**Purpose.** Two things, derived directly from the assessment artefacts: (1) the complete list of UoC requirements/conditions the **AT1 Business Case** assessment covers; (2) the subset of those covered by the **board-presentation event** — the *preparation, delivery, and Q&A* of presenting the business case to the board. (2) is the authoritative scope for the delivery **Topic 5 — "Making the case"** (it replaces the lost `at1-topic-decomposition-draft.md` chunk inventory).

**Sources (2026-06-02).** `assessments/AT1/AT1-BusinessCase-Assessor.docx` (Part A/B marking guides + the "UoC coverage verification" reverse map + the assessor-led Q&A bank) and the three per-unit mapping docs `mappings/ICT{ICT517,CLD401,CLD502}_Assessment_Mapping.docx`.

**Criterion-code key.** AT1 marks against criteria grouped: **A1–A15 = Part A (written Business Case + appendices)** · **B1–B9 = Part B (the observed presentation event)** · **C1–C2 = assessment conditions** (pre-conditions, not student-performed). A UoC item is **presentation-covered iff one of its evidencing criteria is a B-code.**

## Implication for Topic 5 — "Making the case"

Topic 5 must build the skills behind **B1–B9**, across the three phases:
- **Prepare** — turn the written business case into a board deck (B4) and assemble what's tabled to the board (B2/B3).
- **Deliver** — report the case, walk through evaluation, put the ask, in plain English pitched to a board (B1, B2, B3, B4, B7, B9).
- **Handle Q&A** — seek/respond to feedback, listen & question, secure sign-off (B5, B6, B8, B9) — and be ready to **defend the analysis/knowledge** the Q&A probes (the list above), which is *applied* from Topics 1–4, not re-taught.

So the earlier "communication" scoping of Topic 5 is correct and now evidenced: there is **no separate KE-integration chunk** — the only KE in the presentation is *probed* in Q&A (defending Topic 1 knowledge), not newly taught. Topic 5 = prepare → deliver → handle the room.
